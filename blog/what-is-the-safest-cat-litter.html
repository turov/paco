Why do cats need litter?

Cats have the instinct to bury their waste, so they dig a lot in a litter tray before using it. This prevents the spread of the disease from one cat to another. If you keep cat trays clean, your cats will use them regularly - and less likely to begin to use other parts of your home as a restroom!

Why do cats bury their feces?

This is so-called instinctive behavior, which the survival of a cat in the wild mainly depends on. By doing this, cats can hide their smell from predators and protect themselves from being tracked by other animals or people. This behavior also helps them avoid predators who can smell their feces and attack them while they are vulnerable and defenseless.

Is clay litter suitable for your cat?

Clay litter is the most popular type of litter. It is inexpensive and easy to find in pet stores. It absorbs moisture well, but its use also has some disadvantages.

The main drawback of clay is that it practically does not control smells. Since the clay does not stick in a humid state, as other types of litters do, all these wet particles will remain loose and track throughout the house, including where your cats spend most of their time. Is paper litter suitable for your cat?

Some people prefer paper litter because it is environmentally friendly, biodegradable, and easily disposed of. Others may have problems with the smell, dust, or they can find that their cats do not like it at all.

Is silica litter suitable for your cat?

Silica is a natural mineral that absorbs smells, does not contain much dust and is believed to be hypoallergenic. The biodegradable cat litter is made of materials of plant origin, such as corn or olive pits, that decompose faster than a standard clay-based litter.
