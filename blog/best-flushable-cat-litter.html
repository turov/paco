Best Flushable Cat Litter

Let's be honest: no one likes to clean litter boxes. It‚Äôs not really appealing to inhale a pungent smell, sweep the small granules of the litter that scatter on the floor, and carry a heavy plastic bag filled with waste into the garbage bucket.

Despite this, annually, cat owners spend $ 3.9 billion on cat litter. Manufacturers have developed all kinds of products that will help make the cat litter cleaning routine less tiring, from clumping scoopable litters to deodorants and self-cleaning litter boxes. Many manufacturers are already safely stating that you can purchase a litter that can be easily flushed down the toilet.

What is flushable cat litter?

Best flushable cat litter is usually made of biodegradable materials that decompose in the environment. It does not harden when mixed with water, and it can be safely flushed down the toilet.

The usual cat litter is often made from a mixture of clay and silica, whose granules harden and expand when getting wet, giving it a consistency similar to concrete. This can facilitate the cleaning of the litter tray, but it can clog pipes and damage plumbing if you try to throw the waste into the toilet.

Types of flushable cat litter

Best flushable cat litter is often made of biodegradable materials. Here are some of the most common materials used for manufacturing flushable litters:

  • Wheat and corn

    This common agricultural culture is widely used for cat litter production. They are both biodegradable and sustainable. When moisture is added to the starch in wheat and corn, it sticks together, which facilitates the utilization.

  • Wood

    Some brands of flushable cat litter make their products from sawdust, wood chips, and other secondary wood materials (which are not treated with chemical sealants). Thanks to natural deodorants, such as pine and cedar, such litters eliminate smells well.

  • Paper

    The paper flushable cat litter is made from pressed paper granules. Manufacturers often use paper used after consumption to ensure the environmental friendliness of the product. Food soda is often added to the composition to get rid of the smell.

  • Olive pits

    Thanks to the natural composition, the litter made of olive pits can be easily flushed down the toilet, providing a convenient and environmentally friendly option for waste disposal.

The benefits of flushable cat litter

When it comes to cat litter, flushable brands made of environmentally friendly materials offer two large advantages compared to conventional litters: sustainability and ease of disposal.

Sustainability

Currently, the search for a more environmentally friendly alternative to the usual cat litters is one of the main reasons why cat owners choose a flushable cat litter.

Clay, used in traditional cat litters, is collected by means of career production, in which the upper soil and stones are removed to gain access to clay, which affects the ecosystems and pollutes waterways. On the contrary, best flushable kitty litter is made of biodegradable or renewable products.

Convenience of disposal

Flushing garbage down the toilet is much easier than collecting it in a bag and throwing it into trash, especially for cat owners, who have toilet trays in the bathroom or whose street trash tanks are difficult to reach.

Cons of flushable cat litter

A flushable cat litter can be a more environmentally friendly and convenient option, but it also has several disadvantages. Firstly, a clumping litter often does not stick well, which can clog the pipes and cause clogs in the system. Septic systems, in particular, are not designed to remove additional solid particles from the flushable cat litter and can hardly (or are not able) destroy in a septic system.

Secondly, cat feces can contain toxoplasma, a pathogenic parasite that can be transmitted to a person. In an increasing number of marine mammals, toxoplasmosis is diagnosed, which is believed to enter the sea after the owners begin to wash off the litter into the toilet.

Thus, even if the cat litter may have a note "flushable", this does not mean that it can be safely flushed down the toilet. Before disposing of your litter, we recommend that you get acquainted with local laws and legal documents in order to avoid possible problems.
